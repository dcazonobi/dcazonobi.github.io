Intro

I’ve always been obsessed with the story behind the numbers — how data, design, and decisions come together to drive real business outcomes. At my time with Local Kettle Brothers, a luxury watch start up I co-founded, I wasn’t just creating content, I was creating trust. I designed sleek, data-driven visuals that tracked the fluctuating value of timepieces, turning raw market trends into Instagram-worthy charts. Our audience didn’t just see a pretty post, they saw authority. This helped transform us from a small operation into a brand customers felt proud to buy from.

But breaking into data wasn’t smooth. While building my skill set in SQL, Python, and business intelligence tools, I juggled unstable income streams and worked long days delivering for Amazon just to fund my growth. Every free hour went into refining my technical skills, building automations, and exploring how AI could amplify strategy. That period taught me more about discipline, resilience, and execution than any course ever could.

Today, I work as a Data Analyst, bringing together business insight, AI tools, and automated workflows to help companies move smarter. Whether I’m visualising performance, uncovering growth levers, or building strategic dashboards, I operate with the same principle I’ve always had: make data work, make it beautiful, and make it profitable.

Vantage Point Analytics

Trading Strategy For Earnings Events

Objective: VP Analytics provides data and modelling services to hedge funds. This project aimed to test post-earnings drift hypotheses on Apple, Nvidia, and Google to develop a profitable, explainable trading rule for short-term returns.

Methodology:

  • Data Pipeline: Built a robust automated pipeline (auto_data.py) to harmonise price action and macro data, solving complex alignment issues with "After Market Close" announcements.
  • Feature Engineering: Narrowed 44 potential drivers down to 7 high-impact features using Recursive Feature Elimination and Permutation Tests to prevent overfitting.
  • Modeling: Developed a custom backtesting engine to handle the specific nuances of the strategy, prioritizing Cross-Validated R² to ensure "future realism" for client confidence.

Outcome: Delivered two distinct strategies: a "Sniper" model with an 85% hit rate for capital preservation, and a "Workhorse" model generating significantly higher total PnL. The analysis confirmed a "correction" mechanism where pre-earnings rallies often lead to post-event underperformance.

NHS

Expanding Infrastructure & Capacity Analysis

Objective: To analyse whether current NHS GP capacity and staffing levels are sufficient to meet post-pandemic demand. The project investigated appointment availability, attendance rates (DNAs), and resource utilisation across 30 months of data, while also exploring public sentiment via Twitter to gauge real-time system strain.

Methodology:

  • Data Wrangling: Ingested and cleaned three disparate datasets (Regional Appointments, Actual Duration, National Categories) using Pandas. Handled complex date parsing and memory optimisation for millions of records.
  • Analytical Framework: Created a reproducible pipeline to aggregate data by month, region, and healthcare professional type. Calculated key metrics like "System Capacity Utilisation" and daily average volumes.
  • Visualisation: Utilised Seaborn and Matplotlib to map seasonal demand cycles, visualize the "General Practice vs. Other Staff" workload split, and analyse appointment mode preferences (Face-to-Face vs. Telephone).

Outcome: The analysis revealed predictable seasonal demand surges (Autumn/Spring) and a consistent 95% attendance rate, challenging the narrative that DNAs are the primary cause of pressure. It highlighted that non-GP staff scale effectively with demand, suggesting a "flexible workforce" strategy is viable. Recommendations included expanding underutilised video consultations and integrating social sentiment for proactive capacity planning.

Turtle Games

Improving Sales Performance & Customer Loyalty

Objective: Turtle Games sought to improve sales performance by understanding the drivers of customer loyalty. The goal was to build a predictive model for loyalty points, identify distinct customer segments, and formulate a data-driven marketing strategy with specific KPIs.

Methodology:

  • Predictive Modeling: Built a multiple linear regression model using R to predict loyalty points based on spending scores and remuneration. The model achieved a strong R² of 0.83, validating spending and income as key drivers.
  • Customer Segmentation: Utilised K-means clustering (k=5) to categorize the 2,000-customer base into actionable groups, such as "Core Mid/Mid" (the growth lever) and "High-High VIPs" (retention targets).
  • Strategic Planning: Developed a 30/60/90-day action plan, translating analytical findings into specific marketing initiatives like step-up bonuses and VIP tier programs.

Outcome: The analysis provided a clear roadmap to increase sales. Key recommendations included a "step-up bonus" for the large Mid-Mid segment (potentially generating ~69k additional points) and a dedicated retention program for VIPs. The simple, explainable model allowed for "what-if" scenario planning to forecast the impact of income and spending changes on loyalty.

Insurance 4 You

Health Insurance Risk Assessment

Objective: Insurance-4-You (I4U) needed to transition from assumption-based decision-making to a data-driven strategy to optimize profitability. The goal was to analyze client health records, travel preferences, and regional safety data to improve billing accuracy, target family demographics effectively, and refine risk zoning.

Methodology:

  • Data Preparation: Utilized R (tidyverse) to ingest and clean four distinct datasets, ensuring data integrity across health, travel, and police reports.
  • Statistical Analysis: Conducted correlation matrices and normality tests using skimr and moments to validate relationships between variables like BMI, smoking status, and insurance charges.
  • Strategic Modelling: Developed insights based on cross-referencing datasets, such as mapping "suspicious" police incidents against traffic data to create granular risk zones.

Outcome: The analysis corrected a major pricing flaw: High BMI alone does not drive costs—the interaction of Smoking + High BMI does. I recommended a tiered-risk model to prevent undercharging high-risk clients. Additionally, travel data revealed families systematically prefer cars over public transport, leading to a pivoted marketing strategy focusing on "Cost-Efficiency" rather than luxury.

Bright Future

Football Merchandising Opportunity

Objective: BrightFuture aimed to launch a European football team and maximize merchandise profits while strictly adhering to a €150m five-year budget for 17 players. The challenge was to resolve a strategic debate between investing in Spanish vs. Brazilian talent and to identify "high-appeal, low-cost" players who drive fan engagement without the "megastar" price tag.

Methodology:

  • Data Engineering: Ingested and joined SOFIFA (EA Sports) datasets, standardizing player attributes and developing a custom formula to extrapolate weekly wages into a comprehensive 5-year financial projection.
  • Comparative Analysis: Executed SQL queries to benchmark average age, physical metrics, and costs across specific nationalities (Global vs. Brazil vs. Spain), revealing that Brazilian players averaged significantly higher wages and age.
  • Interactive Dashboarding: Built a dynamic Tableau dashboard utilizing Sets and calculated fields to allow stakeholders to "scout" players in real-time, watching the budget impact live as they built their roster.

Outcome: The project delivered a robust decision-support tool that prevented budget overruns. The comparative analysis highlighted that while Brazilian players offered experience, they came at a premium (Avg Age 28, higher wages), whereas other markets offered better long-term ROI. The dashboard successfully enabled the selection of a competitive 17-player squad within the €150m cap.
